## 1. Business Formation

### LLC Formation

- [ ] **Choose state of formation** — Delaware (most flexible for future investors) or your home state (simpler, cheaper for now)
- [ ] **Register LLC** — file Articles of Organization with state Secretary of State
  - Delaware: $90 filing fee + $300/year franchise tax. File at corp.delaware.gov
  - Most other states: $50-500 filing fee
- [ ] **Get EIN** — apply for Employer Identification Number from IRS (free, instant online at irs.gov)
- [ ] **Open business bank account** — separate from personal. Requires EIN + Articles of Organization
- [ ] **Operating Agreement** — draft a basic LLC operating agreement (especially important with 2 founders — define ownership split, roles, decision-making, exit terms)
- [ ] **Register for state sales tax** — required in states where you have nexus (your home state + states where you store inventory)
- [ ] **DBA / "Doing Business As"** — file if your LLC name differs from "Daily Standard" brand name

### Estimated Cost: $150-600

## 2. Product Liability Insurance

### Why You Need It

- Required by most retailers and marketplaces (Amazon, Target)
- Protects against claims of bodily injury, allergic reactions, contamination, or mislabeling
- Protein powder is classified as **low-risk** in the supplement category

### What to Get

| Coverage | Recommended |
|----------|-------------|
| **Product liability** | $1M per occurrence / $2M aggregate (standard) |
| **General liability** | $1M per occurrence / $2M aggregate |
| **Completed operations** | Included in product liability |

### Providers to Contact

| Provider | Notes | Contact |
|----------|-------|---------|
| **Insurance Canopy** | Supplement-specific policies, online quotes | insurancecanopy.com |
| **The Hartford** | Major carrier, dietary supplement coverage | thehartford.com |
| **Castle Rock Agency** | Specialist in supplement/nutraceutical insurance | castlerockagency.com |
| **FLIP (Farmers and Merchants)** | Often used by small DTC brands | fliprogram.com |

### Estimated Cost: $2,800-5,800/year

(For a protein powder brand with <$500K in annual revenue)

### Checklist

- [ ] Get quotes from 2-3 providers
- [ ] Ensure policy covers: product liability, general liability, completed operations
- [ ] Verify coverage includes DTC sales and online marketplaces
- [ ] Confirm coverage territory (USA minimum)
- [ ] Ask about Prop 65 endorsement (California)
- [ ] Purchase policy before first sale

## 3. FDA Compliance

### Classification

Protein powder is a **dietary supplement** under the Dietary Supplement Health and Education Act (DSHEA, 1994). You do NOT need FDA approval to sell. But you must comply with these requirements:

### 3A. Manufacturing (cGMP)

- [ ] **Verify co-packer is cGMP compliant** — must follow 21 CFR Part 111 (Current Good Manufacturing Practices for dietary supplements)
- [ ] **Verify co-packer's facility is FDA-registered** — all facilities that manufacture, process, pack, or hold dietary supplements must register with FDA
- [ ] **Request co-packer's most recent FDA inspection report** — confirm no warning letters or violations

### 3B. New Dietary Ingredient (NDI) Notification

- [ ] **NOT required for Daily Standard** — all proposed ingredients (whey protein isolate, citric acid, natural flavors, stevia, turmeric) were marketed before October 15, 1994 and/or have GRAS (Generally Recognized as Safe) status

### 3C. Facility Registration

- [ ] **If you warehouse product yourself** — register your storage facility with FDA via the FURLS system (fda.gov/food/online-registration-food-facilities)
- [ ] **If 3PL warehouses product** — confirm 3PL facility is FDA-registered
- [ ] **Renewal:** Facility registration must be renewed every two years (October-December of even-numbered years)

### 3D. Adverse Event Reporting

- [ ] **Set up a system to receive and track consumer complaints**
- [ ] **Serious adverse events** must be reported to FDA within 15 business days
- [ ] **Maintain records** of all adverse event reports for 6 years

## 4. Labeling Requirements

### Required Label Elements

Every bag must include the following:

| Element | Requirement | Location |
|---------|-------------|----------|
| **Statement of identity** | "Dietary Supplement" or "Protein Dietary Supplement" | Principal display panel |
| **Net quantity** | Weight in metric (g) and US customary (oz) | Principal display panel |
| **Supplement Facts panel** | FDA-format panel with serving size, servings per container, and all nutrients | Information panel |
| **Ingredient list** | All ingredients in descending order by weight | Below Supplement Facts |
| **Allergen statement** | "Contains: Milk" (whey is a milk-derived ingredient) | Near ingredient list |
| **Name and address** | Company name and place of business (city, state, zip) | Information panel |
| **Lot number** | Batch/lot identifier for traceability | Anywhere on label |
| **Best-by or expiration date** | Optional but recommended | Anywhere on label |

### Supplement Facts Panel Format

```
SUPPLEMENT FACTS
Serving Size: 1 scoop (XXg)
Servings Per Container: 18

                        Amount Per Serving    % Daily Value
Calories                80
Total Fat               0g                    0%
Sodium                  XXmg                  X%
Total Carbohydrate      0g                    0%
Protein                 20g                   40%

Not a significant source of saturated fat, trans fat,
cholesterol, dietary fiber, total sugars, added sugars,
vitamin D, calcium, iron, or potassium.
```

### Structure/Function Claims

If you make claims like "supports muscle recovery" or "promotes daily strength":

- [ ] **File notification with FDA** within 30 days of first marketing
- [ ] **Include disclaimer on label:** *"This statement has not been evaluated by the Food and Drug Administration. This product is not intended to diagnose, treat, cure, or prevent any disease."*
- [ ] **Substantiation:** Must have evidence to support any claims made (keep on file)
- [ ] **File electronically** via FDA's COSM system

### What You CANNOT Say

- No disease claims ("prevents heart disease," "cures inflammation")
- No drug claims ("reduces blood pressure")
- No unapproved health claims
- No misleading images or endorsements

## 5. Third-Party Lab Testing

### Minimum (Required for Trust)

- [ ] **Certificate of Analysis (COA)** for each production batch
  - Protein content verification
  - Amino acid profile
  - Heavy metals (lead, arsenic, cadmium, mercury)
  - Microbiological (E. coli, salmonella, yeast, mold, total plate count)
  - Label accuracy (does the product match what the label says?)
- [ ] **Post COA results on website** (follow Gramms' lead: transparency builds trust)

### Recommended Labs

| Lab | Notes |
|-----|-------|
| **Eurofins** | Major third-party lab, supplement testing |
| **NSF International** | Offers testing without full sport certification |
| **Covance/Labcorp Drug Development** | Comprehensive analytical services |
| **Micro Quality Labs (MQL)** | Supplement-focused, California-based |

### Estimated Cost: $500-1,500 per batch COA

### Optional (V2 / Later)

- [ ] NSF Certified for Sport — required for some retail channels; expensive
- [ ] Informed Sport certification — good for credibility; batch testing
- [ ] BSCG (Banned Substances Control Group) — another option

## 6. California Prop 65

### What It Is

California's Safe Drinking Water and Toxic Enforcement Act requires businesses to warn consumers about exposure to chemicals known to cause cancer or reproductive harm.

### Why It Matters

- You will sell DTC to California residents
- Protein powders can contain trace heavy metals (lead, cadmium) from natural sources
- Your lab testing (COA) will reveal whether your product exceeds Prop 65 thresholds

### Action Items

- [ ] **Review COA results** for heavy metal levels
- [ ] **Compare to Prop 65 thresholds:**
  - Lead: 0.5 mcg/day (no significant risk level)
  - Cadmium: 4.1 mcg/day
- [ ] **If above thresholds:** Add Prop 65 warning to label and website
- [ ] **If below thresholds:** No warning needed (document this for your records)

## 7. Shipping & Fulfillment Compliance

### Shipping Supplements DTC

- [ ] **No special shipping license required** for dietary supplements shipped DTC
- [ ] **Temperature considerations:** Protein powder is shelf-stable; no cold chain needed
- [ ] **State sales tax:** Collect and remit in states where you have nexus. Use Shopify Tax or TaxJar for automation.

### Amazon (If/When You Expand)

- [ ] Amazon requires product liability insurance ($1M minimum)
- [ ] Supplements must comply with Amazon's Dietary Supplement policy
- [ ] COA and third-party testing may be required for listing approval
