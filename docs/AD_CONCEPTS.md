# Daily Standard — 3 Ad Concepts for Testing

**Voice reference:** Trey Kennedy — relatable, observational, clean humor. Sharp takes on everyday dad life. No snark. Feel-good laughs from shared awkwardness. Simple, authentic beats polished.

**Brand:** Irreverent, funny, honest. "Clearly uncool... in a cool way." Steve persona: dad 28–50, works out 2–3x/week, not looking for perfect.

---

## Ad Concept 1: "The Protein Shake Talk"

**Hook:** Dad-to-dad confession. Relatable, observational.

**Format:** 30–60 sec video. Single person to camera (founder or relatable dad). Kitchen or car. Phone-quality, authentic.

**Script / Beat Sheet:**

> *[Dad holds up a protein tub, then a Daily Standard pouch]*
>
> "Used to drink the thick stuff. You know the one. Tastes like chalk. Makes you feel like a bloated balloon for two hours.
>
> Then I had kids. Now it's Tuesday. Or maybe Thursday. And I'm supposed to hit my protein and not feel like garbage?
>
> So we made this. Clear. Like juice. 20 grams. 80 calories. Five ingredients. No artificial sweeteners. No gums. Drinks like something you'd actually want.
>
> Being a dad is hard. At least get your protein in."
>
> *[Shows product in water, drinks]*
>
> **CTA:** Daily Standard. Founding members. Link in bio.

**Why it works:** Directly mirrors Trey Kennedy's "when your dad tries to keep up" energy — observational, self-aware, no hype. Speaks to the shift from "6 days a week" to "whenever I can."

**Test angle:** Problem → solution. Bloating / inconvenience → clear, simple protein.

---

## Ad Concept 2: "The Gym Bro vs. Dad"

**Hook:** Character contrast. Light satire of gym culture vs. real life.

**Format:** 30–45 sec. Quick cuts or split screen. Two characters: Gym Bro (over-the-top) vs. Dad (tired, real).

**Script / Beat Sheet:**

> *[Gym Bro]:* "BRO. You crushing it today? Pre-workout? Post-workout? Intra-workout? Gains, gains, gains—"
>
> *[Dad, deadpan]:* "I have a 6am meeting. I got 20 minutes. I just need protein."
>
> *[Gym Bro]:* "But what about the—"
>
> *[Dad]:* "I don't need a personality. I need 20 grams and 80 calories that doesn't taste like a 2012 chalk shake."
>
> *[Shows Daily Standard — clear, simple]*
>
> "Daily Standard. Clear protein. For the days you train. And the days you don't."
>
> **CTA:** Join founding members. Link in bio.

**Why it works:** Trey Kennedy–style character work. Gym Bro = exaggerated archetype. Dad = grounded, honest. Clean contrast, no mean-spirited tone.

**Test angle:** Anti–gym bro positioning. "Protein, not a personality."

---

## Ad Concept 3: "The Honest Morning"

**Hook:** One real moment. Morning chaos, then one small win.

**Format:** 30–45 sec. Candid morning scene. Kid noise, coffee, rush. Then one quiet beat with the product.

**Script / Beat Sheet:**

> *[Morning. Kitchen. Kid off-screen: "DAD."]*
>
> "Yeah, one second—"
>
> *[Pours water. Adds scoop. Shakes. Sips.]*
>
> "Used to be six days a week. Now it's whenever I can. But I still want to take care of myself. Without it being another full-time job.
>
> This is clear protein. Not a thick shake. 20 grams. 80 calories. Five ingredients. No junk. Mixes like juice.
>
> I drink it every morning. Gym day or not."
>
> *[Kid: "DAD."]*
>
> "Coming."
>
> *[Quick product shot]*
>
> **CTA:** Daily Standard. Founding members. dailystandardprotein.com

**Why it works:** Trey Kennedy's "everyday, normal things" approach. One slice of real dad life. No scripted energy — just a small, honest win.

**Test angle:** Daily habit, not workout fuel. "For the days you train. And the days you don't."

### Image Prompts (Concept 3 — Static Ads)

**Option A: The Messy Morning**
> A millennial dad (30s, tired but calm) sitting on a living room couch, drinking a clear protein drink from a simple glass. He has a slight, resigned smile. In the background: living room chaos — toys scattered, blankets on the floor, breakfast dishes on the coffee table, couch cushions askew. Two little girls (ages 4–7) visible in the background, playing or arguing. Warm morning light through a window. Natural, candid, documentary style. Cream and olive color palette. No gym, no flexing. Real dad life. Photorealistic, editorial photography style.

**Option B: The Beauty Salon**
> A millennial dad (30s) sitting on a living room floor or couch, calmly drinking a clear protein drink from a simple glass. One little girl is painting his fingernails (bright pink or purple polish). Another girl is brushing or styling his hair with a hairbrush. A baby or toddler is tugging on his pant leg. Dad has a deadpan, patient expression — he's clearly surrendered to the moment but still taking his one quiet sip. Warm, natural light. Cream and olive tones. Candid, relatable, slightly humorous. Real family chaos. Photorealistic, editorial style.

## Testing Recommendations

| Concept | Primary message        | Best for              |
|---------|------------------------|------------------------|
| 1. Protein Shake Talk   | Problem/solution, bloating | Broad awareness       |
| 2. Gym Bro vs. Dad      | Anti–gym bro, positioning  | Retargeting, skeptics |
| 3. Honest Morning       | Daily habit, real life     | Warm audiences        |

**Production note:** Trey Kennedy's simpler, authentic content often outperforms highly produced work. Phone-quality, real settings, and natural delivery will likely test better than polished studio spots.

## Additional Ad Ideas

**4. "The Car Moment"**  
Dad in the car before work or school drop-off. One quiet minute with his protein. "This is the only 60 seconds I get." Same honest tone. Tests well for commuters and early risers.

**5. "The Counter Confession"**  
Static image or 15-sec loop. Dad at kitchen counter, protein in hand. Text overlay: "I used to work out 6 days a week. Now it's Tuesday. Or maybe Thursday. At least I'm getting my protein in." Minimal, shareable, meme-adjacent.

**6. "Date Night"**  
Dad and wife on couch, Netflix on. He's drinking his protein. "Date night is the couch. At least my protein is clean." Relatable, self-aware, couples angle.

**7. "The Bloat Comparison"**  
Before/after feeling — not body. "Old protein: bloated for 2 hours. This: drinks like juice. Same 20 grams." Simple problem/solution. Good for retargeting people who've seen the brand.

**8. "Five Ingredients"**  
Extreme close-up of the pouch. Dad voiceover: "Five ingredients. That's it. No artificial sweeteners. No gums. Just protein." Trust-building, ingredient-focused. Good for skeptics.

**9. "The 6am"**  
Dad at 6am, before anyone's up. One quiet moment with his drink. "The only quiet I get." Emotional, aspirational-but-real. Tests well for "me time" seekers.
