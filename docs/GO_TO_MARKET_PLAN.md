# Daily Standard — Go-to-Market Plan

**Date:** February 2026  
**Status:** Pre-launch

---

## 1. Overview

Daily Standard is a clear grass-fed whey protein isolate built for dads. The GTM strategy is founder-led, direct-to-consumer, and designed to validate demand before scaling.

**Approach:** Founder-led. Not influencer. Not anonymous.  
**Narrative:** We built the protein we wanted as busy dads trying to stay strong long term.

## 3. Phase 1: Foundation (Weeks 1–4)

### Business Formation
- Form LLC (Delaware or home state) — $150–600
- Get EIN from IRS (free, instant)
- Open business bank account
- Draft Operating Agreement (ownership, roles, exit terms)
- Register for state sales tax

### Product Liability Insurance
- $1M per occurrence / $2M aggregate
- Providers: Insurance Canopy, The Hartford, Castle Rock Agency
- Estimated cost: $2,800–5,800/year

### Trademark
- Search USPTO for "Daily Standard" in Class 5 + Class 29
- File trademark application ($250–350 per class)

### Brand Finalization
- Select final tagline from 6 candidates
- Lock price point ($49 vs $55 vs $59)
- Finalize V1 flavor profile

## 4. Phase 2: Product (Weeks 4–12)

### Formulation Spec
| Attribute | Spec |
|-----------|------|
| Format | Clear whey protein isolate (juice-like) |
| Protein | 20g per serving |
| Calories | ~80 |
| Fat / Carbs / Sugar | 0g / 0g / 0g |
| Ingredients | 5 max: grass-fed whey isolate, citric acid, natural flavor, stevia, natural color |
| V1 Flavor | Lightly Sweetened Vanilla |
| Packaging | Resealable matte stand-up pouch, 18–20 servings |

### Excluded Ingredients (Non-Negotiable)
- No artificial sweeteners (sucralose, aspartame, acesulfame-K)
- No gums (xanthan, guar, gellan, carrageenan)
- No artificial flavors or colors
- No fillers, no soy lecithin, no maltodextrin, no sugar alcohols

### Vendor Outreach
- Contact 5 co-packers (email template in VENDOR_OUTREACH_EMAIL.md)
- Target: Bactolac, Nutricraft Labs, Daily Manufacturing, Steuart Nutrition, JW Nutritional
- Requirements: cGMP-certified, FDA-registered, R&D support, 1,000–2,500 MOQ
- Follow up after 5 business days if no response

### Sampling & Testing
- Request samples from top 2–3 co-packers
- Evaluate taste, clarity, mixability, sweetness level
- Obtain COA: protein content, heavy metals, microbiology, label accuracy
- Cost: $500–1,500 per batch COA

### Production Run
- First run MOQ: 1,000–2,500 units
- Target cost per unit: $8–15
- Lead time: 8–16 weeks
- Scale target: 5,000–10,000 on subsequent runs

## 5. Phase 3: Pre-Launch (Weeks 10–14)

### Landing Page
- Static HTML/CSS site (deployed to Netlify/Vercel)
- Hero: clear protein positioning, product stats (20g/80cal/5 ingredients/0g sugar)
- Email capture form with founding member discount CTA
- Founder story section
- Connected to Klaviyo or ConvertKit for email collection

### Email Capture Strategy
- CTA: "Be first. Founding members get early access + a discount."
- Goal: 200–500 signups during ad test
- Welcome email sequence (3 emails: welcome, founder story, product details)

### Analytics & Tracking
- Google Analytics or Plausible installed
- Meta Pixel installed and verified (Pixel Helper)
- Custom conversion event for form submission

### Ad Creatives (4 Angles)
Design 1080x1080 (feed) + 1080x1920 (stories) for each:

1. **The Permission:** "You don't need a workout to need protein."
2. **The Dad Reality:** "You used to work out 6 days a week."
3. **The Anti-Hype:** "Protein, not a personality."
4. **The Product:** "20g protein. 80 calories. 5 ingredients. Clear."

## 6. Phase 4: Demand Test (Weeks 14–18)

### Campaign Setup
| Setting | Value |
|---------|-------|
| Platform | Meta (Instagram + Facebook) |
| Objective | Conversions (Lead / Form submission) |
| Daily budget | $25–35/day |
| Duration | 14–28 days |
| Total budget | $500–$1,000 |
| Placement | Automatic (IG Feed, Stories, Reels + FB Feed, Stories) |
| Bid strategy | Lowest cost |

### Audience Targeting
| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Age | 28–50 |
| Gender | Men |
| Location | United States |
| Interests | Protein, Fitness, Health + Parenting, Fatherhood, Family |
| Exclude | Bodybuilding |

### Testing Schedule
- **Week 1–2:** Launch all 4 angles with equal budget. No changes for first 3–4 days.
- **Week 2–3:** Kill underperformers. Shift budget to top 1–2. Test variations of winners.
- **Week 3–4:** Increase budget on winners by 20–30%. Test Lookalike audience if 100+ signups.

### Success Metrics
| Metric | Target | Red Flag |
|--------|--------|----------|
| Cost per lead (CPL) | Under $3–5 | Over $8 |
| Click-through rate (CTR) | Above 1.5% | Below 0.8% |
| Landing page conversion | Above 20% | Below 10% |
| Total signups (2 weeks) | 100–200 | Under 50 |
| Total signups (4 weeks) | 200–500 | Under 100 |

## 7. Phase 5: Decision Framework

| Result | Action |
|--------|--------|
| CPL under $3 + 200+ signups | Strong signal. Move to pre-orders or production. |
| CPL $3–5 + 100+ signups | Good signal. Refine messaging, test new angles. |
| CPL $5–8 | Weak signal. Rethink targeting or creative. |
| CPL over $8 | Pause. Revisit positioning, audience, or offer. |

## 9. Fulfillment Strategy

### Phase 1: Self-Fulfillment
- Ship from home/office
- Use ShipStation or Pirate Ship for label generation
- USPS Priority Mail for orders under 2 lbs
- Best for first 0–500 orders to learn the process

### Phase 2: 3PL (500+ orders/month)
- Evaluate: ShipBob, ShipBots, Relentless Fulfillment
- Requirements: temperature-controlled storage, supplement experience
- Integration with Shopify or WooCommerce

### Shipping Strategy
- Free shipping on orders $75+ (incentivizes bundles)
- Flat rate $5.99 under $75
- US only for V1
